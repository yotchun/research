I am a Researcher in Environmental Engineering focusing on adsorption and coagulation-based separation processes for water treatment.  
My research investigates PFAS and other trace organic contaminants to elucidate adsorption equilibria, kinetics, and competitive interactions in complex water matrices.  
I also study aluminum-based coagulant chemistry and floc formation mechanisms to enhance particle and dissolved organic matter removal.  
By integrating surface chemistry, quantitative modeling, and process engineering, I develop predictive frameworks that link molecular-scale mechanisms to scalable treatment system performance.  
My goal is to translate theory-driven insights into practical and sustainable water treatment technologies.

### Research Focus

- Adsorption equilibria and kinetics
- Aluminum-based coagulant chemistry
- Particle separation mechanisms
- Predictive modeling for treatment processes
